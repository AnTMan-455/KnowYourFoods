Burgers

Burgers are popular fast foods made with a patty and bun, often with cheese, sauces, and toppings. They can provide protein and energy, but frequent intake—especially fast-food versions—may increase health risks because of high sodium, saturated fat, and calories.

🧈 High Saturated Fat
🔥 Moderate–High Calories
⚙️ Processed Ingredients
✅ Better Choice: Grilled / Less Sauce

What is a Burger?

A burger typically consists of a ground meat patty (often beef) served in a sliced bun, commonly paired with lettuce, tomato, onions, pickles, cheese, and sauces. Fast-food burgers may also include processed ingredients and higher sodium levels.

Health Considerations

Burgers can be enjoyed occasionally, but eating fast-food burgers too often may contribute to health concerns such as weight gain and increased risk for heart-related problems—especially when paired with fries and sugary drinks.

Balanced eating tip: If you eat a burger, pair it with water and a healthier side like salad or fruit, and avoid extra sugary sauces.
