Soft Drinks

Soft drinks are sweetened beverages that often contain carbonated water, sugar, flavorings, and sometimes caffeine. They are popular and refreshing, but many soft drinks contain large amounts of added sugar and very little nutritional value.

What are Soft Drinks?

Soft drinks are flavored beverages that usually contain carbonated water, sweeteners, acids, and artificial or natural flavorings. Examples include cola, lemon-lime soda, and orange soda. While they may taste good, they usually do not provide important nutrients like vitamins, minerals, or fiber.

Note: Some soft drinks also contain caffeine, which may affect sleep or cause restlessness in some people.

Tip: Replacing soft drinks with water most of the time can greatly reduce added sugar intake.

Health Considerations

Drinking soft drinks often may contribute to high sugar intake. Choosing water, unsweetened tea, or lower-sugar drinks more often can help support healthier daily habits.

Balanced eating tip: Save soft drinks for occasional treats and drink water during most meals.
