White Bread

White bread is made from refined wheat flour where the bran and germ are removed during processing. This gives the bread a softer texture and longer shelf life, but it also removes much of the fiber and nutrients naturally found in whole grains.

What is White Bread?

White bread is produced from wheat flour that has been refined to remove the bran and germ layers of the grain. This process improves texture and shelf life but reduces fiber, vitamins, and minerals compared to whole grain bread.

Health Considerations

White bread can be part of a balanced diet, but whole grain options are often more nutritious because they contain more fiber, vitamins, and minerals. Fiber helps support digestion and can help maintain stable energy levels.

Balanced eating tip: Try replacing white bread with whole grain bread to increase fiber intake.
