Chicharon

Chicharon is a popular snack made from deep-fried pork skin or pork belly. It is crispy, salty, and commonly eaten as a snack or appetizer. While flavorful, chicharon is typically high in fat, sodium, and calories.

🧈 High Fat
🧂 High Sodium
🔥 High Calories
🥓 Animal Fat
✅ Better Choice: Lean Protein Snacks

What is Chicharon?

Chicharon is made by deep-frying pork skin until it becomes crispy and puffed. It is widely consumed as a snack and is often paired with vinegar or spicy dipping sauces. The frying process gives it its signature texture but also adds significant fat content.

Health Considerations

Chicharon can be enjoyed as an occasional snack, but frequent consumption may contribute to high fat and sodium intake. Moderation and balanced meals can help support better overall health.

Balanced eating tip: Pair salty snacks like chicharon with fresh vegetables or fruit.
