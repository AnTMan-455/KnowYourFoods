Nuggets

Chicken nuggets are small pieces of chicken that are breaded and deep-fried. They are popular fast foods because they are crispy and convenient, but they often contain processed ingredients, added fats, and high sodium levels.

What are Chicken Nuggets?

Chicken nuggets are bite-sized pieces of chicken coated with batter or breadcrumbs and cooked by deep frying. Many fast-food nuggets are made from processed chicken mixtures rather than whole chicken pieces. They are often eaten with sauces such as ketchup, barbecue sauce, or mayonnaise.

Tip: The healthiest choice is usually grilled chicken rather than fried nuggets.

Health Considerations

Chicken nuggets can be enjoyed occasionally, but eating fried and processed foods too often may increase fat and sodium intake. Choosing grilled chicken or limiting sauces can help maintain a more balanced diet.

Balanced eating tip: Pair nuggets with water and fruits or vegetables instead of sugary drinks and fries.
