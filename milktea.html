Milk Tea

Milk tea, often called bubble tea or boba tea, is a sweet beverage made with tea, milk, sugar, and chewy tapioca pearls. While it is a popular drink, many milk tea varieties contain high amounts of sugar and calories.

What is Milk Tea?

Milk tea is a beverage made by combining brewed tea with milk or cream and sweeteners. Many versions also include tapioca pearls, jelly, or flavored syrups. While it can be refreshing, the added sugar and toppings can increase the calorie content significantly.

Tip: Choosing lower sugar levels or skipping pearls can significantly reduce calories.

Health Considerations

Milk tea can be enjoyed occasionally, but frequent intake of sugary drinks may increase sugar consumption. Choosing smaller portions and less sweet options can help maintain a balanced diet.

Balanced eating tip: Try ordering milk tea with less sugar or no pearls.
