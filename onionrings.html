Onion Rings

Onion rings are slices of onion coated in batter or breadcrumbs and deep-fried until crispy. They are commonly served as a side dish with burgers and other fast foods. While onions themselves contain nutrients, frying them in oil increases the calorie and fat content.

What are Onion Rings?

Onion rings are made by slicing onions into rings, dipping them in batter or breadcrumbs, and frying them until crispy. They are often served with dipping sauces such as ketchup, mayonnaise, or barbecue sauce. Even though onions naturally contain fiber and vitamins, frying them adds extra oil and calories.

Health Considerations

Onion rings can be enjoyable as an occasional snack or side dish, but eating fried foods too often may increase fat and calorie intake. Choosing baked versions or balancing meals with vegetables and fruits can help support healthier eating habits.

Balanced eating tip: Pair fried foods with water and a vegetable side instead of sugary drinks and additional fried items.
