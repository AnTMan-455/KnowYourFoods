Fries from Street Vendors

Street vendor fries are deep-fried potato strips commonly sold as affordable snacks. They are often served with cheese powder, sauces, or seasonings, making them flavorful but also high in calories, sodium, and oil.

What are Street Vendor Fries?

Street fries are typically made from potatoes that are cut and deep-fried in oil. They are popular in many places because they are cheap, convenient, and tasty. However, the frying process adds a large amount of oil, which increases calories and fat content.

Health Considerations

Fries can be enjoyed occasionally, but frequent consumption of deep-fried foods may contribute to high fat and sodium intake. Choosing baked or grilled alternatives can help create a more balanced diet.

Balanced eating tip: If you eat fries, pair them with water and a protein source instead of sugary drinks.
