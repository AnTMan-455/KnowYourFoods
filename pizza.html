Pizza

Pizza is a well-known food made with dough, sauce, cheese, and different toppings. It can provide energy, protein, and calcium, but many fast-food or heavily topped pizzas may also be high in sodium, saturated fat, and calories.

What is Pizza?

Pizza is usually made from a baked flatbread dough topped with tomato sauce, cheese, and ingredients like pepperoni, ham, mushrooms, onions, or bell peppers. It is popular because of its rich flavor and variety, but the nutritional value depends a lot on the crust, toppings, portion size, and amount of cheese.

Tip: Pizza gets much heavier in calories when you add extra cheese, stuffed crust, and processed meat toppings. Thin crust and veggie toppings are usually better choices.

Health Considerations

Pizza can be enjoyed as part of a balanced diet, but eating it too often—especially with sugary drinks and large portions—may contribute to excess calorie intake. Choosing smaller portions, thinner crust, and more vegetable toppings can make it a better option.

Balanced eating tip: If you eat pizza, pair it with water and add fruit or salad on the side instead of more processed snacks.
